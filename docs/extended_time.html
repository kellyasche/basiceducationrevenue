Extended Time


Description

Beginning in 2004, school districts are prohibited from counting a student as more than 1.0 in average daily membership, which they used to do if a student was participating in a learning year program (summer school, extended day, extended week, etc..). This program allows a school district to count a student who participates in extended programming for up to an additional 0.2 students in ADM for the time the student spends in summer school, etc…. The allowance is $5,117 X the district’s extended time adjusted pupil units.

State Legislation

126C.10 Subd. 2.Basic revenue.

Subd. 2a.Extended time revenue.

  1. A school district’s extended time revenue is equal to the product of $5,117 and the sum of the adjusted pupil units of the district for each pupil in average daily membership in excess of 1.0 and less than 1.2 according to section 126C.05, subdivision 8.

  2. Extended time revenue for pupils placed in an on-site education program at the Prairie Lakes Education Center or the Lake Park School, located within the borders of Independent School District No. 347, Willmar, for instruction provided after the end of the preceding regular school year and before the beginning of the following regular school year equals membership hours divided by the minimum annual instructional hours in section 126C.05, subdivision 15, not to exceed 0.20, times the pupil unit weighting in section 126C.05, subdivision 1, times $5,117.

  3. A school district’s extended time revenue may be used for extended day programs, extended week programs, summer school, vacation break academies such as spring break academies and summer term academies, and other programming authorized under the learning year program.

The distribution ……



Since the last official data is from 2022, we will use that fiscal year to analyze the distribution of revenue.

Extended time is a horizontal funding mechanism. Therefore it is distributed on a per student basis. In this case, it’s based on students that attend that participates in programs outside of the typical school day. Therefore, each school district will receive varying amounts depending on how many students they have participating in these programs, but on a per student basis, the revenue should equal $5,117 for each school district, RUCA category, and Region.

All the tables below indicate that each district does receive $5,117 per extended time pupil units. However, on just an adjusted pupil unit basis the revenue does vary. This indicates that schools have varying proportion of students that are participating in extended learning programs.

The district table below shows that the districts receiving the most extended time revenue per APU vary in terms of being metro or rural districts. The highest is Dover-Eyota with $452 per APU. The other districts in the top are a mixture of metro and rural districts.
