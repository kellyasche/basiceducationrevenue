Transition


Description

Transition revenue provides school districts with a partial grandfather or hold-harmless due to the 2003 Legislature’s changes to general education revenue. Transition revenue guarantees a school district the lesser of

  • it’s fiscal year 2003 general education revenue per pupil, or
  • the amount of revenue per pupil that the district would have received during the 2004 fiscal year under the old definitions of general education revenue.

The difference between the actual fiscal year 2004 revenue and the guaranteed amount is the new transition revenue.

This revenue was provided entirely in state aid for fiscal year 2004 and is an equalized aid and levy for later years. Transition revenue is provided through an equalized aid and levy based on a referendum market value equalizing factor of $510,000.


State Legislation

Subd. 31.Transition revenue.

  1. A district’s transition allowance equals the sum of the transition revenue the district would have received for fiscal year 2015 under Minnesota Statutes 2012, section 126C.10, subdivisions 31, 31a, and 31c, and the greater of zero or the difference between:
    1. the sum of:
      1. the general education revenue the district would have received for fiscal year 2015 according to Minnesota Statutes 2012, section 126C.10;
      2. the integration revenue the district received for fiscal year 2013 under Minnesota Statutes 2012, section 124D.86;
      3. the pension adjustment the district would have received for fiscal year 2015 under Minnesota Statutes 2012, section 127A.50;
      4. the special education aid the district would have received for fiscal year 2015 under Minnesota Statutes 2012, section 125A.76; and
      5. the special education excess cost aid the district would have received for fiscal year 2015 under Minnesota Statutes 2012, section 125A.79; and
    2. the sum of the district’s:
      1. general education revenue for fiscal year 2015 excluding transition revenue under this section;
      2. achievement and integration revenue for fiscal year 2015 under section 124D.862;
      3. special education aid for fiscal year 2015 under section 125A.76; and
      4. alternative teacher compensation revenue for fiscal year 2015 under section 122A.415, divided by the number of adjusted pupil units for fiscal year 2015.
  2. A district’s transition revenue for fiscal year 2015 and later equals the product of the district’s transition allowance times the district’s adjusted pupil units.

Subd. 32.Transition levy.

To obtain transition revenue for fiscal year 2015 and later, a district may levy an amount not more than the product of its transition revenue for the fiscal year times the lesser of one or the ratio of its referendum market value per resident pupil unit to $510,000.

Subd. 33.Transition aid.

  1. For fiscal year 2004, a district’s transition aid equals its transition revenue.
  2. For fiscal year 2005 and later, a district’s transition aid equals its transition revenue minus its transition levy times the ratio of the actual amount levied to the permitted levy.


Data

There are three parts to this formula.

  1. Revenue: hhrev
  2. Levy: hhlvy
  3. Aid: hhaid

Revenue - hhrev

hhrev = hhalo x awadm22

  • hhalo = school districts transition allowance
  • awadm22 = school districts adjusted pupil units

Levy - hhlvy

hhlvy = hhrev x ((rmkv19 / wadm22) / hh_eq_factor)

  • hhrev = school district’s transition revenue
  • rmkv19 = school district’s referendum market value
  • wadm22 = school district’s resident pupil units
  • hh_eq_factor = $510,000

Once this is calculated, a comparison is made to see which is lesser, revenue or levy amounts - the hhlvy value is changed to whichever is the smaller value. Therefore, if the revenue is smaller than the levy, the aid (calculated next) would be $0.

Aid - hhaid

hhaid = hhrev - hhlvy
